ABOUT ME

Hi, my name is Joshua Duarte! I am a maker, a designer, and a recent graduate of Texas A&M University where I obtained my B.S. in Mechanical Engineering. During my time there, I had the pleasure of interning at Northrop Grumman, ExxonMobil, and The Boeing Company. Through these experiences I learned about organizational leadership, additive and subtractive manufacturing, and further expanded my understanding of design processes. I also served as the President of the Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers where I led a team of 8 others in growing the organization to more than 400 members. Through these experiences I have grown to believe in relentless learning, and in my quest for personal growth I've completed projects ranging from an automated intelligent chessboard, a sign language interpreter, to a planner incorporating machine learning. I believe that these projects led me to search for my next challenge: obtaining my masters at U.C. Berkeley, where I am now embarking on an interdisciplinary, team-based capstone project aimed at creating an omni-directional treadmill to enhance a user's virtual reality experience.

BUTLR

A planner that allow users to easily input tasks, events, and habits to then export to a variety of calendar applications. Attempting to incorporate machine learning within Python programming to effectively plan tasks and habits

Machine Learning: Gravitational Effects on Boiling

Used machine learning to explore the interplay of gravity and Marangoni effects during boiling of a water/2-propanol mixture for a range of gravity acceleration conditions.

Sign Language Tracker

Using OpenCV, mediapipe, and tensorflow, this project detects gestures in ones hand that resemble those in the sign language alphabet. The end goal is to see the sign language words per minute speed of the user.

Scissor Lift

A scissor lift that was aimed to familiarize creators with additive and subtractive manufacturing methods like that of 3D printing, lathe operations, mill operations, and other metal working tools. Ultimately held 6 kg with less than 3 millimeters of deflection.

The Right Left

The right left is a sensor based system that was created during a 24-hour hackathon that tackles the dreaded unprotected left hand turns. This system tracked the speed of oncoming traffic and would use pre-measured data to inform the driver.

Fermii

An iOS based application that would allow users to know when and where their fellow classmates would be studying the same material. This would encourage people to work with one another and learn through teaching. Not only would one benefit from learning from one another, but would be rewarded for it too.
